Cybersecurity self-evaluation

Answer 12 questions to assess your current cybersecurity practices. There are no right or wrong answers.Choose the option that best matches your organization today.

Step 1 of 3

Govern & Identify

1) Do you assign cybersecurity responsibility (even part-time) to a specific role/person?

Category: GOVERN

2) Do you have simple cybersecurity rules/policies (passwords, access, updates, data handling)?

Category: GOVERN

3) Do you keep an up-to-date list of key IT assets (laptops, servers, cloud services, critical software)?

Category: IDENTIFY

4) Do you know what data is most important (customer data, financial data, IP) and where it is stored?

Category: IDENTIFY

Protect & Detect

5) Do you use multi-factor authentication (MFA) on important accounts (email, admin, banking, cloud)?

Category: PROTECT

6) Are devices and software updated quickly (security patches for OS, apps, browsers)?

Category: PROTECT

7) Do you have basic monitoring for suspicious activity (alerts, logs, unusual sign-ins, includes basic alerts from email or cloud services)?

Category: DETECT

8) Do staff know how to report suspicious emails or unusual device behavior?

Category: DETECT

Respond & Recover

9) Do you have a simple incident plan (what to do if hacked, who to call, what to disconnect)?

Category: RESPOND

10) If an incident happens, do you know how to communicate internally and to customers/partners (if needed)?

Category: RESPOND

11) Do you back up important data and verify you can restore it?

Category: RECOVER

12) After issues occur (outages, near-misses), do you review what happened and improve processes?

Category: RECOVER
Please answer all questions before viewing results.

Answering tips

  • Pick what’s true today, not what you want it to be.
  • If unsure, choose the option that best reflects everyday practice.

    About this project

    This website is a student-built cybersecurity self-evaluation created to help small organizations think about their current cybersecurity practices. It is for educational use and does not replace a professional security assessment.

    Why the focus on SMEs under 50 employees

    This project focuses on organizations with fewer than 50 employees, which aligns with the European Union definition of micro and small enterprises. Companies in this size range often face resource constraints, such as limited time, budget, and access to cybersecurity expertise.

    NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

    The NIST Cybersecurity Framework is a widely used structure for managing cybersecurity risk across organizations of different sizes and sectors. It organizes cybersecurity activities into six core functions that help organizations understand and improve their security posture.

    GOVERN

    Establish responsibility, policies, and oversight.

    IDENTIFY

    Understand assets, data, and risks.

    PROTECT

    Apply safeguards such as MFA, patching, and access control.

    DETECT

    Identify suspicious activity and potential incidents.

    RESPOND

    Take action during cybersecurity incidents.

    RECOVER

    Restore operations and improve resilience.

    Framework simplification for SMEs

    The NIST CSF can be detailed and resource-intensive to implement. This project simplifies the framework into 12 practical questions (two per function), focusing on controls that are realistic for small organizations. The goal is to provide an accessible and easy method for SMEs to do a quick check. It is not a full audit, but a simple starting point.
